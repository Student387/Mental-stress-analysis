Sleep Improvement

Sleep hygiene practices for better rest and mental health

Quality sleep is essential for cognitive function, emotional regulation, and stress management. Poor sleep can worsen stress and anxiety.

Sleep Hygiene Guidelines
  • Consistent Schedule: Go to bed and wake up at the same time each day, including weekends.
  • Screen-Free Hour: Avoid screens (phones, laptops) at least 1 hour before sleep.
  • Environment: Keep your room cool, dark, and quiet.
  • Limit Caffeine: Avoid caffeine after 2 PM.
  • No Heavy Meals: Avoid large meals close to bedtime.
Additional Tips
  • Expose yourself to natural light during the day
  • Use relaxation techniques (e.g., deep breathing) before bed
  • Reserve your bed for sleep only; avoid studying in bed
Helpful Resources
  • Sleep Foundation – Evidence-based sleep hygiene tips
  • Calm – Sleep stories and wind-down exercises
